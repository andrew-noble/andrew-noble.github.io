Adventure

The above section is mainly an effort to make money to enable this section. I love adventure in many forms. I love adventure outside the norm, I love adventure in a storm. I love it here and I love it there, I love adventures everywhere. My dad also read green eggs and ham to me as a kid.

Pacific Crest Trail 2021

My formative adventure. The best summer of my life as I spent 4 months walking a dusty path through Calfornia, Oregon and Washington. Through smoke and heat, frost and pain, I realized that not only can you be supremely happy in spite of being dirty, tired, and hungry, you might find yourself happy because of those things. The image on the left was taken by my talented friend Nathan Hurner.

There is an image album at the link below. I used bootstrap to make that, for practice of course.

Pfiffner Traverse July 2023

My long-planned, very first high route from Rocky Mountain National Park to I-70. An amazing 5-day solo adventure full of wildflowers, navigation skill building and thunder- and hail-based humblings. Probably the greatest bang-for-my-buck adventure I have ever done.

Oak Nightstand

My first major woodworking project was this little oak nightstand made in winter 2022. It is an uncharacteristically nice first project because of the terrifically well-equipped makerspace in Longmont, CO. The front is patterned plywood, I learned that in this Youtube video.

Bikepacking Stembags

In winter 2023 I made my first textile project: these stem bags for bikepacking. I learned from the pattern on MYOG, which has a bunch of other cool stuff too.

PCT Monuments

I made this pair of miniature PCT monuments to commemorate my 2021 hike. Even though simple, these are still one of my favorite projects. I used a table saw, mitre saw, and a lazer engraver.
